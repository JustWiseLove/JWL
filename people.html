People of the Bible

BIBLE FIGURES

1. Jehovah - The Almighty God, Creator of all things, who revealed Himself as the one true God to His people (Genesis 1:1; Exodus 3:14). He is the source of all life and the one who establishes covenants with humanity (Psalm 83:18).

BIBLE FIGURES

2. Jesus Christ - The Son of God, sent to earth to redeem humanity through His sacrificial death (John 3:16; Matthew 16:16). He is the appointed King of God’s Kingdom and the mediator between God and man (1 Timothy 2:5).

BIBLE FIGURES

3. Moses - The leader chosen by God to deliver the Israelites from Egypt and receive the Law at Mount Sinai (Exodus 3:10; Deuteronomy 34:10). He is a mediator of the covenant between God and Israel (Exodus 20:1-17).

BIBLE FIGURES

4. Abraham - The father of many nations, known for his faith in God’s promise to make his descendants a great nation (Genesis 12:1-3; Hebrews 11:8). God made a covenant with him, promising the land of Canaan (Genesis 17:8).

BIBLE FIGURES

5. David - A king after God’s own heart, who ruled Israel and was an ancestor of Jesus (1 Samuel 16:13; Acts 13:22). He wrote many Psalms and established Jerusalem as the center of worship (2 Samuel 5:9).

BIBLE FIGURES

6. Noah - A righteous man who built the ark as God commanded, preserving his family and animals during the Flood (Genesis 6:9; Hebrews 11:7). God made a covenant with him, symbolized by the rainbow (Genesis 9:13).

BIBLE FIGURES

7. Paul - An apostle who spread the good news to the Gentiles, writing many letters in the New Testament (Acts 9:15; Romans 1:1). He endured great hardships to preach Christ (2 Corinthians 11:23-27).

BIBLE FIGURES

8. Peter - A disciple of Jesus, known as the "rock" on which Christ would build His church (Matthew 16:18; John 21:15-17). He was a leader among the apostles and preached at Pentecost (Acts 2:14).

BIBLE FIGURES

9. Solomon - David’s son, known for his wisdom and building the first temple in Jerusalem (1 Kings 3:12; 1 Kings 6:1). He wrote Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, and Song of Solomon (Proverbs 1:1).

BIBLE FIGURES

10. Isaiah - A prophet who foretold the coming of the Messiah and God’s Kingdom (Isaiah 7:14; Isaiah 9:6-7). His prophecies encouraged Judah during times of distress (Isaiah 1:1).

BIBLE FIGURES

11. John the Baptist - The forerunner of Jesus, who prepared the way by preaching repentance and baptizing (Matthew 3:1-3; Luke 1:17). He baptized Jesus in the Jordan River (Matthew 3:13-16).

BIBLE FIGURES

12. Mary (Mother of Jesus) - The virgin chosen to give birth to Jesus, showing great faith and humility (Luke 1:30-38; Matthew 1:18). She was present at the crucifixion (John 19:25).

BIBLE FIGURES

13. Joseph (Jesus’ Earthly Father) - The carpenter who accepted Mary and raised Jesus as his own, protecting the family as God directed (Matthew 1:20-24; Luke 2:16). He took them to Egypt to escape Herod (Matthew 2:13-14).

BIBLE FIGURES

14. Adam - The first man created by God, who lived in Eden but fell into sin, bringing death to humanity (Genesis 2:7; Genesis 3:17-19). His disobedience affected all his descendants (Romans 5:12).

BIBLE FIGURES

15. Eve - The first woman, created as a companion for Adam, who was deceived by the serpent and ate the forbidden fruit (Genesis 2:22; Genesis 3:6). She is the mother of all living (Genesis 3:20).

BIBLE FIGURES

16. Daniel - A prophet who served in Babylon, known for his faithfulness and interpreting dreams (Daniel 2:19-23; Daniel 6:10). He survived the lions’ den through God’s protection (Daniel 6:22).

BIBLE FIGURES

17. Elijah - A prophet who challenged the prophets of Baal and demonstrated God’s power on Mount Carmel (1 Kings 18:36-39). He was taken to heaven in a whirlwind (2 Kings 2:11).

BIBLE FIGURES

18. Elisha - Elijah’s successor, who performed miracles and served as a prophet in Israel (2 Kings 2:9-12; 2 Kings 4:32-35). He received a double portion of Elijah’s spirit (2 Kings 2:9).

BIBLE FIGURES

19. John (the Apostle) - A disciple of Jesus who wrote the Gospel of John, three epistles, and Revelation (John 13:23; Revelation 1:1). He was exiled to Patmos (Revelation 1:9).

BIBLE FIGURES

20. Jeremiah - A prophet who warned Judah of its impending destruction and wrote the book of Lamentations (Jeremiah 1:5; Lamentations 1:1). He faced much opposition (Jeremiah 20:7-9).

BIBLE FIGURES

21. Joshua - Moses’ successor who led the Israelites into the Promised Land, conquering Jericho (Joshua 1:1-3; Joshua 6:20). He was a faithful servant of God (Joshua 24:15).

BIBLE FIGURES

22. Samuel - A prophet and judge who anointed Saul and David as kings of Israel (1 Samuel 3:20; 1 Samuel 16:13). He served as a priest and seer (1 Samuel 9:9).

BIBLE FIGURES

23. Isaac - Abraham’s son, the child of promise through whom God’s covenant continued (Genesis 21:1-3; Genesis 26:4). He was nearly sacrificed by Abraham (Genesis 22:9-12).

BIBLE FIGURES

24. Jacob - Isaac’s son, who became Israel and fathered the twelve tribes (Genesis 32:28; Genesis 35:10). He wrestled with an angel and received a blessing (Genesis 32:24-29).

BIBLE FIGURES

25. Joseph (Son of Jacob) - Sold into slavery but became a ruler in Egypt, saving his family during a famine (Genesis 37:28; Genesis 41:41). He forgave his brothers (Genesis 50:20).

BIBLE FIGURES

26. Esther - A Jewish queen who risked her life to save her people from genocide in Persia (Esther 4:14; Esther 7:3-4). Her courage led to their deliverance (Esther 8:17).

BIBLE FIGURES

27. Ruth - A Moabite who showed loyalty to her mother-in-law Naomi and became an ancestor of David (Ruth 1:16; Ruth 4:17). Her story highlights faithfulness (Ruth 2:11).

BIBLE FIGURES

28. Deborah - A prophetess and judge who led Israel to victory against the Canaanites (Judges 4:4; Judges 4:14). She was a wise and courageous leader (Judges 5:7).

BIBLE FIGURES

29. Gideon - A judge who led a small army to defeat the Midianites with God’s help (Judges 6:11-14; Judges 7:7). He initially doubted but grew in faith (Judges 6:36-40).

BIBLE FIGURES

30. Samson - A judge with great strength who fought the Philistines but struggled with personal weaknesses (Judges 13:5; Judges 16:17). He died while destroying a Philistine temple (Judges 16:30).

BIBLE FIGURES

31. Job - A righteous man who endured immense suffering but remained faithful to God (Job 1:1; Job 1:21). God restored his fortunes after his trials (Job 42:10).

BIBLE FIGURES

32. Nehemiah - A governor who led the rebuilding of Jerusalem’s walls despite opposition (Nehemiah 2:17; Nehemiah 6:15). He also restored worship practices (Nehemiah 8:8).

BIBLE FIGURES

33. Ezra - A priest and scribe who led a return to Jerusalem and taught the Law to the people (Ezra 7:10; Ezra 9:4). He helped restore proper worship (Ezra 10:11).

BIBLE FIGURES

34. Ezekiel - A prophet who received visions of God’s glory and warned Judah of its destruction (Ezekiel 1:1; Ezekiel 3:17). He spoke of future restoration (Ezekiel 36:24).

BIBLE FIGURES

35. Hosea - A prophet who symbolized God’s love for Israel by marrying an unfaithful woman (Hosea 1:2; Hosea 3:1). He called Israel to repentance (Hosea 14:1).

BIBLE FIGURES

36. Jonah - A prophet who fled from God’s command but eventually preached to Nineveh, leading to their repentance (Jonah 1:1-3; Jonah 3:10). He was swallowed by a great fish (Jonah 1:17).

BIBLE FIGURES

37. Aaron - Moses’ brother, who served as the first high priest of Israel (Exodus 4:14; Exodus 28:1). He made the golden calf but later repented (Exodus 32:4).

BIBLE FIGURES

38. Miriam - Moses’ sister, who helped save him as a baby and later led the women in worship (Exodus 2:4-7; Exodus 15:20). She was punished for challenging Moses (Numbers 12:10).

BIBLE FIGURES

39. Caleb - A faithful spy who believed God’s promise about the Promised Land (Numbers 13:30; Joshua 14:6-12). He inherited Hebron for his faithfulness (Joshua 14:14).

BIBLE FIGURES

40. Rahab - A Canaanite woman who hid Israelite spies and was spared during Jericho’s fall (Joshua 2:1-3; Joshua 6:25). She became an ancestor of Jesus (Matthew 1:5).

BIBLE FIGURES

41. Boaz - A kinsman-redeemer who married Ruth and became an ancestor of David (Ruth 2:1; Ruth 4:13). He showed kindness and generosity (Ruth 2:10).

BIBLE FIGURES

42. Hezekiah - A king of Judah who trusted God and led a religious reform (2 Kings 18:5; 2 Chronicles 29:2). God extended his life by 15 years (2 Kings 20:6).

BIBLE FIGURES

43. Josiah - A king of Judah who discovered the Law and led a revival of true worship (2 Kings 22:8; 2 Kings 23:25). He destroyed idols and restored the Passover (2 Kings 23:21).

BIBLE FIGURES

44. Timothy - A young disciple mentored by Paul, who became a leader in the early church (1 Timothy 1:2; Acts 16:1). Paul wrote two letters to him (2 Timothy 1:5).

BIBLE FIGURES

45. Barnabas - A companion of Paul who encouraged early Christians and supported missionary work (Acts 4:36; Acts 13:2). He helped reconcile Paul and the church (Acts 9:27).

BIBLE FIGURES

46. Luke - A physician and companion of Paul who wrote the Gospel of Luke and Acts (Colossians 4:14; Luke 1:1-4). He recorded early church history (Acts 1:1).

BIBLE FIGURES

47. Matthew - A tax collector turned disciple of Jesus, who wrote the Gospel of Matthew (Matthew 9:9; Matthew 10:3). His gospel focuses on Jesus as the Messiah (Matthew 1:1).

BIBLE FIGURES

48. Mark - A companion of Peter and Paul, who wrote the Gospel of Mark (Acts 12:12; Mark 1:1). His gospel emphasizes Jesus’ actions (Mark 10:45).

BIBLE FIGURES

49. James (Son of Zebedee) - A disciple of Jesus and brother of John, who was martyred for his faith (Matthew 4:21; Acts 12:2). He witnessed Jesus’ transfiguration (Mark 9:2).

BIBLE FIGURES

50. Andrew - Peter’s brother and a disciple of Jesus, who brought others to Christ (John 1:40-42; Matthew 4:18). He was a fisherman by trade (Mark 1:16).

BIBLE FIGURES

51. Philip - A disciple of Jesus who brought Nathanael to Him and was involved in the feeding of the 5,000 (John 1:43-45; John 6:5-7). He later preached in Samaria (Acts 8:5).

BIBLE FIGURES

52. Thomas - A disciple known for doubting Jesus’ resurrection until he saw Him (John 20:24-29). He later declared Jesus as Lord and God (John 20:28).

BIBLE FIGURES

53. Nathanael (Bartholomew) - A disciple whom Jesus called a true Israelite without deceit (John 1:47; Matthew 10:3). He witnessed Jesus’ miracles (John 21:2).

BIBLE FIGURES

54. Judas Iscariot - The disciple who betrayed Jesus for 30 pieces of silver and later hanged himself (Matthew 26:14-16; Matthew 27:5). He was replaced by Matthias (Acts 1:26).

BIBLE FIGURES

55. Mary Magdalene - A follower of Jesus who was freed from demons and witnessed His crucifixion and resurrection (Luke 8:2; John 20:1-18). She announced His resurrection to the disciples (John 20:18).

BIBLE FIGURES

56. Martha - A friend of Jesus who served Him but was corrected for being overly anxious (Luke 10:40-41; John 11:1). She expressed faith in Jesus’ power (John 11:21-22).

BIBLE FIGURES

57. Lazarus - Martha and Mary’s brother, whom Jesus raised from the dead (John 11:1; John 11:43-44). His resurrection strengthened many people’s faith (John 12:10-11).

BIBLE FIGURES

58. Stephen - The first Christian martyr, who was stoned for his bold testimony about Jesus (Acts 6:8; Acts 7:59-60). His death marked the beginning of persecution (Acts 8:1).

BIBLE FIGURES

59. Silas - A companion of Paul who joined him on missionary journeys and was imprisoned with him (Acts 15:40; Acts 16:25). He helped strengthen early churches (Acts 16:40).

BIBLE FIGURES

60. Priscilla - A woman who, with her husband Aquila, taught Apollos and hosted a church in their home (Acts 18:2; Acts 18:26). She supported Paul’s ministry (Romans 16:3).

BIBLE FIGURES

61. Aquila - Priscilla’s husband, who worked with Paul as a tentmaker and supported early Christian communities (Acts 18:2-3; Romans 16:3). He traveled with Paul (Acts 18:18).

BIBLE FIGURES

62. Apollos - An eloquent preacher who was taught by Priscilla and Aquila and became a powerful teacher (Acts 18:24-26; 1 Corinthians 3:6). He ministered in Corinth (Acts 19:1).

BIBLE FIGURES

63. Titus - A companion of Paul who served as a leader in the church at Crete (Titus 1:4-5; 2 Corinthians 8:23). Paul wrote a letter to him (Titus 1:1).

BIBLE FIGURES

64. Philemon - A Christian who owned a slave named Onesimus, whom Paul encouraged him to forgive (Philemon 1:1; Philemon 1:10-16). Paul wrote a letter to him (Philemon 1:1).

BIBLE FIGURES

65. Onesimus - A runaway slave who became a Christian and was reconciled to his master Philemon through Paul (Philemon 1:10; Colossians 4:9). He served Paul in prison (Philemon 1:13).

BIBLE FIGURES

66. Lydia - A seller of purple goods who became a Christian and hosted Paul in Philippi (Acts 16:14-15; Acts 16:40). She was the first European convert (Acts 16:14).

BIBLE FIGURES

67. Cornelius - A Roman centurion who became the first Gentile convert, receiving the holy spirit (Acts 10:1; Acts 10:44-45). His conversion opened the door for Gentiles (Acts 10:34-35).

BIBLE FIGURES

68. Ananias (of Damascus) - A disciple who restored Paul’s sight after his conversion (Acts 9:10-18). He was initially hesitant but obeyed God (Acts 9:13-14).

BIBLE FIGURES

69. Dorcas (Tabitha) - A disciple known for her good works, whom Peter raised from the dead (Acts 9:36; Acts 9:40). Her restoration brought many to faith (Acts 9:42).

BIBLE FIGURES

70. Eutychus - A young man who fell asleep and died during Paul’s sermon but was revived (Acts 20:9-10). His restoration encouraged the believers (Acts 20:12).

BIBLE FIGURES

71. Felix - A Roman governor who heard Paul’s defense but kept him imprisoned to please the Jews (Acts 24:22-27). He was fearful but did not act (Acts 24:25).

BIBLE FIGURES

72. Festus - A governor who succeeded Felix and heard Paul’s case, sending him to Caesar (Acts 25:1; Acts 25:12). He found no fault in Paul (Acts 25:25).

BIBLE FIGURES

73. Agrippa - A king who heard Paul’s defense and acknowledged his innocence (Acts 26:1; Acts 26:31-32). He was almost persuaded to become a Christian (Acts 26:28).

BIBLE FIGURES

74. Nicodemus - A Pharisee who visited Jesus at night and later helped bury Him (John 3:1-2; John 19:39). He defended Jesus before the Sanhedrin (John 7:50-51).

BIBLE FIGURES

75. Joseph of Arimathea - A secret disciple who provided a tomb for Jesus’ burial (Matthew 27:57-60; John 19:38). He boldly asked Pilate for Jesus’ body (Mark 15:43).

BIBLE FIGURES

76. Zacchaeus - A tax collector who climbed a tree to see Jesus and repented after meeting Him (Luke 19:2-5; Luke 19:8). Jesus dined at his house (Luke 19:7).

BIBLE FIGURES

77. Bartimaeus - A blind man healed by Jesus, who called out to Him in faith (Mark 10:46-52). His healing demonstrated Jesus’ compassion (Mark 10:52).

BIBLE FIGURES

78. Jairus - A synagogue leader whose daughter Jesus raised from the dead (Mark 5:22-23; Mark 5:41-42). His faith in Jesus was rewarded (Mark 5:36).

BIBLE FIGURES

79. Joanna - A woman who supported Jesus’ ministry and witnessed His resurrection (Luke 8:3; Luke 24:10). She was among the women at the tomb (Luke 24:1).

BIBLE FIGURES

80. Salome - A follower of Jesus who witnessed His crucifixion and visited His tomb (Mark 15:40; Mark 16:1). She supported His ministry (Mark 15:41).
