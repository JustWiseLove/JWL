Creation’s Cycles Teach Trust

Rhythms of Nature Show Reliability

Nature’s cycles—seasons, tides, migrations, orbits—demonstrate Jehovah’s dependable truth, as “As long as the earth remains, seedtime and harvest, cold and heat, summer and winter, day and night will not cease.” Their precision fosters trust in His eternal order. — Genesis 8:22

Tidal Harmony: The moon’s gravity cycles tides every 12.4 hours, moving 1.4 trillion tons of water, as “You made the moon to mark the appointed times.” Tides sustain 50% of marine life, cleaning 71% of earth. — Psalm 104:19

Animal Migrations: Monarch butterflies migrate 3,000 miles via sunlight, as “How great are your works, O Jehovah!” Arctic terns travel 25,000 miles, guided by stars. With 99% accuracy, migrations show divine care. — Psalm 92:5

Planetary Orbits: Earth’s 66,600 mph orbit, 93 million miles from the sun, maintains 15–30°C, as “He has established the earth on its foundations.” Orbits vary by 0.0001%, ensuring stability. — Psalm 104:5

Earth’s Rotation: Earth’s 1,000 mph rotation creates 24-hour days, as “He made the moon to mark the appointed times; the sun knows when to set.” Rotation balances 14.7 psi atmosphere, supporting 8.7 million species. — Psalm 104:19

Practical Steps: Track seasons, observe tides, watch migrations, study orbits, or note day-night cycles. Plant crops, journal rhythms, and trust Jehovah’s order, as “Wait for Jehovah.” — Psalm 27:14
