Jehovah

The Sacred Name of the Creator

Why the Name Matters

Jehovah’s name, appearing over 7,000 times in Scripture, is the key to true worship. Meaning “He Causes to Become,” it reveals His role as the dynamic Creator. “I am Jehovah; that is my name” (Isaiah 42:8).

Revelation to Moses

In 1513 BCE, Jehovah told Moses, “This is my name forever” (Exodus 3:15), tying His identity to His promise of a paradise earth.

Jesus’ Teaching

Jesus prayed, “Let your name be sanctified” (Matthew 6:9), and said, “I have made your name known” (John 17:26), showing its importance for salvation.

Historical Suppression

Satan’s efforts to erase Jehovah’s name began at Babel (2269 BCE) and continued with Jewish scribes replacing YHWH with “Lord” around 300 BCE. Yet, finds like the Dead Sea Scrolls (200 BCE) confirm its use.

Fate vs. Free Will

Satan’s lie of fate, rooted in Babylonian astrology (2000 BCE), denies choice. Jehovah offers free will: “Choose life so that you may live” (Deuteronomy 30:19). Nineveh’s repentance (830 BCE) proves His mercy.

Satan’s Lies

From Eden’s “You will not die” (Genesis 3:4) to evolution and hellfire, Satan blinds humanity. Jehovah’s truth frees: “You will know the truth” (John 8:32).

“You will call me and pray to me, and I will listen to you.” —Jeremiah 29:12

Creation

The Work of Jehovah’s Hands

A Purposeful Design

Jehovah created through Jesus, “the firstborn of all creation” (Colossians 1:15). The earth’s tilt, magnetic field, and water cycle show it was “formed to be inhabited” (Isaiah 45:18).

Cambrian Explosion

Complex life appeared suddenly 540 million years ago, with no evolutionary precursors, supporting divine creation over Darwin’s theory.

Human Genome

With 3 billion DNA base pairs, its complexity defies chance, pointing to Jehovah’s wisdom: “You have made all of them in wisdom” (Psalm 104:24).

The Flood

In 2370 BCE, Jehovah’s global flood destroyed a corrupt world. Fossils in the Karoo Formation and flood legends worldwide confirm this cataclysm.

Animal Creation

Animals were made “according to their kinds” (Genesis 1:25). Features like the eagle’s eye and bat’s echolocation show irreducible complexity, not gradual evolution.

“In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth.” —Genesis 1:1

True Worship

Serving Jehovah with Spirit and Truth

A Way of Life

True worship means doing Jehovah’s will: “Not everyone saying ‘Lord, Lord,’ will enter the Kingdom” (Matthew 7:21). It’s “taking in knowledge” of Him (John 17:3).

Persecution’s Endurance

In 64 CE, Nero tortured Christians, yet they rejoiced, “counted worthy to be dishonored” (Acts 5:41), spreading the good news further.

Global Mission

Jehovah’s people preach worldwide: “This good news of the Kingdom will be preached in all the earth” (Matthew 24:14).

Good News

The Kingdom’s message promises a paradise: “The righteous will possess the earth” (Psalm 37:29). It’s been preached since Jesus’ day (Luke 8:1).

Baptism

A public dedication, baptism is for adults who choose Jehovah: “Go, make disciples, baptizing them” (Matthew 28:19), not infants as false religions teach.

Neutrality

True worshipers avoid politics and wars: “My Kingdom is no part of this world” (John 18:36), a stand that mirrors Daniel’s loyalty (Daniel 6:10-16).

“You must worship Jehovah your God with spirit and truth.” —John 4:24

False Worship

Satan’s Counterfeit Religion

Babylon the Great

False worship, “Babylon the Great” (Revelation 17:5), began at Babel (2269 BCE) and will fall: “Get out of her, my people” (Revelation 18:4).

Pagan Origins

Christmas stems from Saturnalia (217 BCE), and Easter from Eostre’s fertility rites (8th century CE), both rejected by Jehovah: “Do not learn the way of the nations” (Jeremiah 10:2).

Clergy System

Titles like “Reverend” mimic pagan hierarchies. Jesus said, “You are all brothers” (Matthew 23:8), making all worshipers equal.

Trinity Lie

Formalized in 325 CE, the Trinity echoes Babylonian triads (2000 BCE). Jehovah says, “I am one Jehovah” (Deuteronomy 6:4); Jesus is “the firstborn” (Colossians 1:15).

Spiritism

Contacting the dead is demonic: “There should not be found in you anyone who inquires of the dead” (Deuteronomy 18:11). The dead “know nothing” (Ecclesiastes 9:5).

Reincarnation

Hinduism’s cycle (1200 BCE) denies Jehovah’s resurrection: “There is going to be a resurrection” (Acts 24:15), not endless lives.

“Their idols are silver and gold, the work of human hands.” —Psalm 115:4

The Kingdom

Jehovah’s Government for Earth

A Real Government

Jesus rules since 1914: “The God of heaven will set up a kingdom” (Daniel 2:44). It will end human rule and bring paradise.

1914’s Signs

World War I, famines, and earthquakes mark “the last days” (Matthew 24:7), confirming Christ’s enthronement after 2,520 years from 607 BCE.

Armageddon

Jehovah’s war will end wickedness: “The kings of the earth are gathered” but defeated (Revelation 19:19-21), ushering in peace.

Prophetic Evidence

Daniel’s statue (Daniel 2:31-44) and Ezekiel’s “overturning” (Ezekiel 21:27) pinpoint 1914, when empires fell, signaling God’s Kingdom.

Last Days

Since 1914, “critical times” (2 Timothy 3:1) like wars and immorality show the end nears: “The great day of Jehovah is near” (Zephaniah 1:14).

“Your Kingdom is an everlasting kingdom.” —Psalm 145:13

The Memorial

Honoring Jesus’ Sacrifice

A Sacred Observance

Held on Nisan 14, the Memorial recalls Jesus’ death: “Keep doing this in remembrance of me” (Luke 22:19), the “Lamb of God” (John 1:29).

New Covenant

Jesus’ blood seals the covenant: “This cup means the new covenant” (Luke 22:20), offering forgiveness and life.

Jesus’ Role

As “the firstborn of all creation” (Colossians 1:15), Jesus is “the way and the truth” (John 14:6), mediator to Jehovah.

Significance

The Memorial fulfills the Passover: “Christ our Passover lamb has been sacrificed” (1 Corinthians 5:7), a ransom for many.

Mediator

Jesus bridges man to God: “One mediator between God and men” (1 Timothy 2:5), uniting all through his sacrifice.

“This cup means the new covenant by virtue of my blood.” —1 Corinthians 11:25

Family

Jehovah’s Design for Unity

A Sacred Bond

Marriage is Jehovah’s plan: “They will become one flesh” (Genesis 2:24). Families teach His ways: “Train up a boy” (Proverbs 22:6).

Roles Defined

Husbands lead lovingly, wives support, children honor: “Love your wives as Christ loved” (Ephesians 5:25).

Morality Upheld

Jehovah demands holiness: “You must be holy” (1 Peter 1:16). Sodom’s fall (1918 BCE) warns against immorality.

Conscience

The conscience guides morally: “Their conscience is bearing witness” (Romans 2:15). Training it ensures “love out of a clean heart” (1 Timothy 1:5).

“Love your wife as yourself.” —Ephesians 5:33

Paradise

Jehovah’s Promise Restored

An Earthly Hope

Paradise restores Eden: “The righteous will live forever on it” (Psalm 37:29). Jesus’ ransom reverses Adam’s curse (1 Peter 1:18-19).

Salvation Earned

Faith and works save: “The one who endures to the end will be saved” (Matthew 24:13), for the meek, not heaven-bound.

Judgment Day

Jehovah judges justly: “Vengeance is mine” (Deuteronomy 32:35). Armageddon ends evil, sparing the righteous (Zephaniah 2:3).

Resurrection

The dead rise: “All those in the tombs will come out” (John 5:28-29), judged by deeds, not an afterlife (Revelation 20:12-13).

Death’s Truth

Death is sleep, not afterlife: “The dead know nothing” (Ecclesiastes 9:5). Jehovah promises resurrection, not myths (Hosea 13:14).

Hell Exposed

Hell is the grave, not torment: “Sheol is silence” (Ecclesiastes 9:10). Satan’s lie collapses before Jehovah’s love (1 John 4:8).

“The righteous will possess the earth, and they will live forever on it.” —Psalm 37:29
“Ponder over these things; be absorbed in them, so that your advancement may be plainly seen by all people.” —1 Timothy 4:15-16
