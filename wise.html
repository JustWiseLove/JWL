WISE

Proverbs 4:7 “Wisdom is the most important thing.”

Spiritual necessities equip the faithful to serve Jehovah with a heart full of wisdom and faith.

Spiritual Food

Nourish the Heart with Truth

Regular Bible study feeds the spirit for Jehovah’s service, as “man must live, not on bread alone, but on every word from Jehovah” (Matthew 4:4). Spiritual food builds faith.

Biblical Guidance: Ezra studied Jehovah’s law diligently (Ezra 7:10). Jesus taught scripture as food (John 6:35). Neglecting study weakens faith, as “faith follows the things heard” (Romans 10:17).

Practical Steps: Study daily, meditate on scriptures, and apply truths, as “your word is a lamp to my foot” (Psalm 119:105).

“Man must live on every word from Jehovah.” — Matthew 4:4

Spiritual Work of Prayer

Commune with Jehovah

Prayer, a vital spiritual work, strengthens service, as “pray constantly” (1 Thessalonians 5:17). It deepens the relationship with Jehovah.

Biblical Examples: Hannah prayed fervently, receiving Samuel (1 Samuel 1:10-20). Jesus prayed before ministry (Luke 6:12-13). Prayer sustains service, as “Jehovah is near to all who call” (Psalm 145:18).

Practical Steps: Pray regularly, seek Jehovah’s guidance, and express gratitude, as “cast your burden on Jehovah” (Psalm 55:22).

“Pray constantly.” — 1 Thessalonians 5:17

Spiritual Rest

Find Peace in Jehovah

Spiritual rest, through trust in Jehovah, renews the heart for service, as “the peace of God will guard your hearts” (Philippians 4:7). Peace calms the soul.

Biblical Insight: David found rest in Jehovah amid trials (Psalm 23:1-3). Jesus offered peace to disciples (John 16:33). Trust prevents anxiety, as “do not be anxious over anything” (Philippians 4:6).

Practical Steps: Meditate on Jehovah’s promises, trust His care, and find calm, as “he gives rest to the weary” (Jeremiah 31:25).

“Peace I leave with you.” — John 16:33

Spiritual Community

Encourage One Another

Spiritual support from the congregation strengthens service, as “consider one another to incite to love” (Hebrews 10:24-25). Fellowship builds faith.

Biblical Examples: Early Christians met regularly, uplifting each other (Acts 2:42). Paul encouraged Timothy (2 Timothy 1:5-7). Unity aids service, as “love one another” (John 13:34).

Practical Steps: Attend meetings, encourage others, and share faith, as “build one another up” (1 Thessalonians 5:11).

“Build one another up.” — 1 Thessalonians 5:11

Spiritual Preparation

Sharpen Discernment

Spiritual preparation, like building discernment, equips for service, as “discretion will guard you” (Proverbs 2:11). Wisdom protects faith.

Biblical Guidance: Solomon sought wisdom for judgment (1 Kings 3:9-12). Paul urged discernment (Philippians 1:9-10). Preparation aids service, as “guard your heart” (Proverbs 4:23).

Practical Steps: Study scriptures, reflect on principles, and seek wisdom, as “the wisdom from above is pure” (James 3:17).

“Discretion will guard you.” — Proverbs 2:11

Spiritual Balance

Zeal with Wisdom

Spiritual balance, zeal without fanaticism, sustains service, as “do not let your zeal lack wisdom” (Romans 12:11). Balance reflects maturity.

Biblical Insight: Apollos was zealous yet learned accuracy (Acts 18:24-26). Jesus taught with balance (Matthew 11:19). Balance aids service, as “be peaceable with all” (Romans 12:18).

Practical Steps: Cultivate zeal, seek counsel, and act wisely, as “wisdom is with the modest” (Proverbs 11:2).

“Be fervent in spirit.” — Romans 12:11

Spiritual Endurance

Resist Temptation

Spiritual endurance through temptation strengthens service, as “happy is the man who keeps on enduring trial” (James 1:12). Faith overcomes evil.

Biblical Examples: Joseph resisted Potiphar’s wife (Genesis 39:7-12). Jesus overcame Satan’s temptations (Matthew 4:1-11). Endurance aids service, as “God will not let you be tempted beyond what you can bear” (1 Corinthians 10:13).

Practical Steps: Pray for strength, avoid temptation, and trust Jehovah, as “he will deliver you” (Psalm 34:19).

“God will make the way out.” — 1 Corinthians 10:13

Gratitude for Blessings

Thankfulness Strengthens Faith

Gratitude for spiritual blessings, like truth and hope, fuels service, as “let the peace of the Christ rule in your hearts” (Colossians 3:15). Thankfulness deepens love.

Biblical Insight: The psalmist praised Jehovah’s truth (Psalm 119:160). Paul thanked God for hope (Romans 15:13). Gratitude drives service, as “offer to God a sacrifice of praise” (Hebrews 13:15).

Practical Steps: Reflect on blessings, express thanks, and serve joyfully, as “rejoice in the hope” (Romans 12:12).

“Offer to God a sacrifice of praise.” — Hebrews 13:15
