SYMPOSIUM: Messianic Prophecy Fulfilled! — Part I

1. Acknowledged by God

Scriptures: Psalm 2:7; Matthew 3:16, 17; Acts 13:33, 34

Notes:

  • Illustration: A 31-story building on Padre Island, Texas (2006), collapsed due to a weak foundation, showing faith needs a strong foundation to endure trials.
  • Foundation of Faith:
    • 1 Corinthians 3:11: “For no one can lay any other foundation than what is laid, which is Jesus Christ.”
    • Jesus is the Messiah, head of the congregation, and King of God’s Kingdom.
    • Surface-level belief is insufficient; trust in Jesus must be deep.
  • Illustration: Choosing a babysitter requires proven trustworthiness. Similarly, trust in Jesus grows through studying fulfilled prophecies.
  • Prophecy:
    • Psalm 2:7: “You are my son; Today I have become your father.”
    • Isaiah 42:1: Jehovah would support and place His spirit on His servant.
  • Fulfillment:
    • Matthew 3:16-17: At Jesus’ baptism, Jehovah declared, “This is my Son, the beloved, whom I have approved.”
    • Matthew 17:5: At the transfiguration, Jehovah repeated this.
    • Acts 13:33-34: Resurrection fulfilled Psalm 2:7.
  • Why Remarkable?:
    • First time Jehovah’s voice was heard directly.
    • John 1:32-34: John the Baptist witnessed the spirit and voice.
    • Resurrection: Jehovah’s greatest miracle, witnessed by over 500 (1 Corinthians 15:6).
  • Application: Meditating on this prophecy strengthens faith in Jesus as the Messiah, encouraging us to preach His Kingship.

2. Descended From King David

Scriptures: 2 Samuel 7:12, 13; Matthew 1:1, 2, 6

Notes:

  • Introduction: Genealogy searches satisfy curiosity, but Jesus’ ancestry fulfills Messianic prophecies.
  • Prophecy:
    • 2 Samuel 7:12-13: David’s offspring would have an everlasting kingdom.
    • Genesis 22:18: Abraham’s offspring would bless all nations.
  • Fulfillment:
    • Matthew 1:1-2, 6: Jesus’ legal descent through Solomon to Joseph.
    • Luke 3:23: Natural descent through Nathan to Mary.
  • Why Remarkable?:
    • Genealogies span thousands of years, preserved despite the 607 BCE destruction.
    • Divine protection ensured the Messianic line.
  • Application: Jesus’ lineage confirms His Messiahship, encouraging preaching.

3. Anointed as “Messiah the Leader”

Scriptures: Daniel 9:25; Luke 3:1, 2, 21-23

Notes:

  • Prophecy:
    • Daniel 9:25: 69 weeks from Jerusalem’s rebuilding to the Messiah.
    • Daniel 9:26: City and temple destroyed after Messiah’s death (70 CE).
  • Fulfillment:
    • Nehemiah 2:1, 7-8: Decree in 455 BCE (Artaxerxes’ 20th year).
    • 483 years (69 weeks) to 29 CE, when Jesus was baptized (Luke 3:1-2, 21-23).
  • Why Remarkable?:
    • Precise timing fulfilled in 29 CE.
    • Signs built expectation (e.g., angels, Simeon).
    • Matthew 28:18: Jesus received all authority.
  • Application: The prophecy’s precision confirms Jesus as the Messiah, encouraging us to follow Him.

Who Really Rules the World?

Scriptures: Mark 12:17; Luke 4:5-8; John 18:36

Notes:

  • Humanity’s Failure: War (over 250 conflicts since 1945), hunger (733 million in 2025), and disease persist.
  • Satan’s Authority:
    • Luke 4:5-8: Satan’s temporary control since Adam and Eve’s rebellion.
    • John 12:31: Jesus called Satan “ruler of this world.”
  • Satan’s Tactics:
    • Ephesians 2:2: Promotes nationalism, racism, division.
    • Manipulates governments, prioritizing power.
  • Proper View of Governments:
    • Romans 13:1: Submit to authorities for order.
    • Proverbs 21:1: Jehovah can direct rulers’ hearts.
    • Titus 3:1: Respect authorities, enhancing Jehovah’s reputation.
  • Maintaining Pure Worship:
    • Mark 12:17: “Pay back Caesar’s things to Caesar, but God’s things to God.”
    • John 18:36: Christians remain neutral, avoiding politics.
  • Video Examples:
    • Herbert Senior (1916): Refused military service, endured imprisonment.
    • Carrie Lewis (1940): Refused flag salute, faced assault.
    • Malawian Witnesses (1967, 1975): Refused political cards despite persecution.
  • Conclusion: Satan’s rule will end (John 12:31). Imitate Jesus and faithful Witnesses by maintaining neutrality.

Overall Application

  • Strengthening Faith: Fulfilled prophecies confirm Jesus as the Messiah and King, building an unshakable foundation of faith.
  • Action:
    • Study and meditate on prophecies to deepen trust in Jesus.
    • Share evidence of Jesus’ Messiahship through preaching.
    • Maintain neutrality and exclusive worship of Jehovah.
  • Hope: Jehovah’s promises, fulfilled through Jesus, assure a future free from Satan’s influence, with God’s Kingdom resolving all problems.
  • Next Steps: Anticipate Part 2 of the symposium (Saturday afternoon) for more prophecies confirming Jesus’ role.
