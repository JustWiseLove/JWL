The Memorial of Christ’s Death

The Memorial of Christ’s death, observed annually on Nisan 14, is the most sacred event for true worshipers, commemorating Jesus’ sacrifice that provides salvation, as he said, “This cup means the new covenant by virtue of my blood, which is to be poured out in your behalf” (Luke 22:20). Instituted in 33 CE during the Passover, Jesus replaced the lamb with unleavened bread and wine, symbols of his body and blood, instructing, “Keep doing this in remembrance of me” (Luke 22:19). This event fulfills Jehovah’s purpose, “By means of him we have the release by ransom through his blood” (Ephesians 1:7), offering life to obedient mankind, “The Son of man came to give his life as a ransom for many” (Matthew 20:28). Only the 144,000 anointed partake of the emblems, “Unless you eat the flesh of the Son of man and drink his blood, you have no life in yourselves” (John 6:53), while millions attend to show gratitude, “Observe the month of Abib and celebrate the Passover to Jehovah your God” (Deuteronomy 16:1). The date, tied to the Jewish lunar calendar, ensures precision, as “Christ our Passover lamb has been sacrificed” (1 Corinthians 5:7).

Satan’s false religions distort this event with pagan rituals like Easter, blending Christ’s resurrection with fertility rites traced to Babylon around 2000 BCE, but Jehovah’s Word clarifies, “You cannot be partaking of the table of Jehovah and the table of demons” (1 Corinthians 10:21). The Memorial’s simplicity—bread, wine, prayer—rejects such lies, reflecting Jesus’ humility, “He poured out his life even to death” (Isaiah 53:12). Its annual observance, like the Passover’s deliverance from Egypt in 1513 BCE, “This day will serve as a memorial for you” (Exodus 12:14), underscores Jehovah’s mercy, “God loved the world so much that he gave his only-begotten Son” (John 3:16). True worshipers prepare diligently, “Let us celebrate the festival, not with old leaven, but with unleavened bread of sincerity and truth” (1 Corinthians 5:8), honoring Christ’s sacrifice that upholds Jehovah’s justice and love.

Passover Connection

The Memorial is rooted in the Passover, established when Jehovah spared Israel’s firstborn in Egypt, “When I see the blood, I will pass over you” (Exodus 12:13), a deliverance celebrated annually, “You must keep it as a festival to Jehovah” (Exodus 12:14). Jesus, “the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world” (John 1:29), fulfilled this type, dying on Nisan 14, 33 CE, as “our Passover lamb” (1 Corinthians 5:7). The bread and wine replace the lamb, signifying his perfect sacrifice, “By one offering he has perfected forever those who are being sanctified” (Hebrews 10:14). This connection shows Jehovah’s consistent purpose, “I am Jehovah; I do not change” (Malachi 3:6), linking past deliverance to future salvation, “Through him you believe in God, who raised him up from the dead” (1 Peter 1:21).

This distinction refutes false teachings that all partake, ensuring “we must all appear before the judgment seat of the Christ” (2 Corinthians 5:10).
