Isaiah 43:10 “You are my witnesses,” declares Jehovah, “my servant whom I have chosen.”

Abel, Enoch, Noah: First Witnesses

(Pre-Flood Era, c. 4000–3000 BCE)

The earliest faithful servants, valued as treasures, witnessed for Jehovah’s righteousness in a corrupt world, proclaiming His truth with unwavering faith, as “the righteous will be remembered forever” (Psalm 112:6).

Abel: Adam’s son, Abel offered a sacrifice pleasing to Jehovah, testifying to his faith through obedient worship (Genesis 4:4). Biblical accounts record his offering as “by faith,” earning divine approval (Hebrews 11:4). Though murdered by Cain, Abel’s witness endures, as his blood “cries out” (Genesis 4:10).

Enoch: Enoch “walked with God,” boldly prophesying against wickedness (Jude 14-15). Biblical texts note his preaching of divine judgment, and Jehovah “took him” to spare him death (Genesis 5:24; Hebrews 11:5), affirming his loyalty to Jehovah’s truth.

Noah: A “preacher of righteousness” (2 Peter 2:5), Noah built the ark and warned of the Flood for 120 years (Genesis 6:3). Facing ridicule, he upheld Jehovah’s laws, saving his family (Hebrews 11:7). His witness is preserved in ancient Near Eastern flood accounts.

“By faith Abel offered God a better sacrifice than Cain.” — Hebrews 11:4

Moses, Samuel, Jeremiah: Prophetic Witnesses

(15th–6th Century BCE)

Israel’s prophets, as treasured servants, proclaimed Jehovah’s name and laws, guiding His people to true devotion, as “the word of Jehovah endures” (1 Peter 1:25).

Moses: Raised in Egypt, Moses led Israel from slavery, receiving the Law at Sinai, declaring “Jehovah your God is one Jehovah” (Deuteronomy 6:4). He proclaimed Jehovah’s name to Pharaoh (Exodus 3:15) and taught Israel to worship Him alone (Exodus 20:3), as biblical accounts confirm.

Samuel: As prophet and judge, Samuel anointed Saul and David, urging Israel to serve Jehovah exclusively (1 Samuel 12:10-20). His preaching, preserved in Hebrew texts, strengthened devotion to Jehovah, as “serve Jehovah with all your heart” (1 Samuel 12:24).

Jeremiah: Jeremiah warned Judah of Babylon’s judgment, proclaiming “Jehovah is the true God” (Jeremiah 10:10). Imprisoned and mocked, he rewrote his burned scrolls (Jeremiah 36:32), testifying to Jehovah’s enduring word, as biblical records show.

“This is my name forever.” — Exodus 3:15

Jesus, Peter, Paul: Apostolic Witnesses

(1st Century CE)

Jesus and His apostles, as priceless treasures, established Christian witnessing, proclaiming Jehovah’s Kingdom with steadfast faith, as “the truth will set you free” (John 8:32).

Jesus Christ: Jesus declared “I have made your name known” (John 17:26), teaching Jehovah’s purpose through parables and miracles (Matthew 4:17). He preached publicly and house-to-house (Luke 8:1), enduring death on a stake to uphold Jehovah’s sovereignty (Philippians 2:8), as biblical accounts record.

Peter: A leading apostle, Peter preached at Pentecost, declaring Jehovah’s resurrection of Jesus (Acts 2:32-36). Imprisoned and beaten, he continued witnessing (Acts 5:42), stating “we must obey God rather than men” (Acts 5:29), as preserved in early manuscripts.

Paul: Converted from persecutor, Paul traveled thousands of miles, establishing congregations and writing 14 epistles (Acts 9:15). Beheaded under Nero, his letters, in biblical manuscripts, proclaim “Jehovah is my helper” (Hebrews 13:6).

“Go, make disciples of all the nations.” — Matthew 28:19

Polycarp, Justin Martyr: Early Martyrs

(1st–4th Century CE)

Early Christians, like treasured gems, witnessed for Jehovah amid persecution, preserving His truth, as “be faithful even to death” (Revelation 2:10).

Polycarp of Smyrna: A disciple of John, Polycarp refused to honor false gods, declaring, “I am a Christian.” Burned at the stake in 155 CE, his martyrdom, recorded in historical texts, upheld Jehovah’s worship, as “the word of Jehovah endures” (1 Peter 1:25).

Justin Martyr: A philosopher-turned-Christian, Justin wrote *Apologies* defending Christianity, proclaiming Jehovah as the true God. Beheaded in 165 CE, his works, in historical archives, testified to Jehovah’s sovereignty.

Perpetua: Executed in 203 CE, Perpetua faced arenas, declaring Jehovah’s Kingdom. Her courage, documented in historical records, strengthened early Christian witness.

“The word of our God endures forever.” — Isaiah 40:8

Waldensians, Wycliffe, Tyndale, Hus

(12th–16th Century CE)

Medieval and Reformation translators, as treasured servants, proclaimed Jehovah’s Word, making it accessible despite opposition, as “those seeking Jehovah will praise him” (Psalm 22:26).

Waldensians: Led by Peter Waldo in the 12th century, the Waldensians translated vernacular Bibles, sharing Jehovah’s truth with the poor. Persecuted, they hid manuscripts in the Alps, as historical records confirm, proclaiming “worship with spirit and truth” (John 4:24).

John Wycliffe: Wycliffe’s English Bible (1382) from the Latin Vulgate gave access to Jehovah’s Word. Declared a heretic, his bones were burned after death, but his translations, in the Bodleian Library, Oxford, spread truth, as “Your word is a lamp to my foot” (Psalm 119:105).

Jan Hus: Hus preached Jehovah’s truth, rejecting false doctrines. Burned at the stake in 1415, his writings, in historical archives, inspired reform, as “the truth will set you free” (John 8:32).

William Tyndale: Tyndale’s Pentateuch (1530) used “Jehovah” (Exodus 6:3). Strangled and burned in 1536, his translations, in the British Library, London, proclaimed Jehovah’s name.

“Your word is truth.” — John 17:17

Henry Grew: Grew taught “Jehovah” as God’s name and rejected false doctrines in the 1820s. His pamphlets, in historical archives, reached many, as “the dead know nothing at all” (Ecclesiastes 9:5).

George Storrs: Storrs taught earthly resurrection and no immortal soul through *Six Sermons* in the 1840s. Persecuted, his writings, in the British Museum, London, spread Jehovah’s truth, as “the meek will possess the earth” (Psalm 37:11).

“My people will know my name.” — Isaiah 52:6

Charles Taze Russell, Bible Students

(Late 19th Century)

Charles Taze Russell, a treasured servant, organized systematic witnessing, proclaiming Jehovah’s Kingdom, as “this good news of the Kingdom will be preached” (Matthew 24:14).

Charles Taze Russell: In 1870, Russell formed a Pittsburgh Bible study, rejecting false doctrines. Launching *Zion’s Watch Tower* in 1879, he published truth, distributing millions of tracts. His *Studies in the Scriptures* taught Jehovah’s purpose, and he founded the Watch Tower Bible and Tract Society (1884). Calculating 1914 as Christ’s enthronement (Daniel 4:25, 607 BCE–1914), confirmed by World War I (Matthew 24:7), he faced opposition. His works, in the British Museum, London, restored Jehovah’s name, as “You are my witnesses” (Isaiah 43:10).

Bible Students: Followers like Maria Ackley and Alexander Macmillan preached door-to-door, facing mob violence. Their conventions, like Cedar Point 1919, rallied thousands to proclaim Jehovah’s truth, as “declare his glory among the nations” (1 Chronicles 16:24).

“I did not hold back from preaching.” — Acts 20:20

Rutherford, Knorr, Modern Witnesses

Naming the group Jehovah’s Witnesses in 1931 (Isaiah 43:10), he used radio broadcasts to share truth, as “proclaim his name among the peoples” (Psalm 105:1).

Nathan H. Knorr: From 1942, Knorr oversaw the New World Translation (1950–1961), restoring “Jehovah” 7,237 times. Founding Gilead School (1943), he trained missionaries for 240 lands. His leadership, in historical archives, expanded witnessing, as “Your word is truth” (John 17:17).
