(1st–2nd Century CE)

Historical records document this drift toward Greek philosophy, obscuring Jehovah’s name, as “they exchanged the truth of God for the lie” (Romans 1:25).

Despite this, Jehovah’s Word endured, as “not one word of all his good promise failed” (1 Kings 8:56). The Septuagint (200 BCE), preserving “YHWH,” safeguarded Jehovah’s name, fulfilling “the word of Jehovah endures forever” (1 Peter 1:25). This apostasy set the stage for further corruption, but Jehovah promised, “My people will know my name” (Isaiah 52:6).
