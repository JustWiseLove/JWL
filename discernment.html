Mormonism: False Scriptures and Polytheism

Works-based salvation through secret temple rituals, practiced since the 1830s in Nauvoo, opposes Ephesians 2:8-9’s grace by faith. They celebrate Christmas (Saturnalia, 4th-century CE) and Easter, ignoring 2 Corinthians 6:17’s call to separate. Missionary work, limited to young men for two years, fails Matthew 24:14’s universal preaching command. Polygamy, practiced until 1890, and closed temple ceremonies, unlike Jesus’ open teachings (John 18:20), stray from Bible truth. Baptisms for the dead and focus on genealogy lack Scriptural basis, diverting from Jehovah’s worship (John 4:23). Mormonism’s wealth, with billions in assets, and corporate structure contrast Jesus’ humility (Matthew 8:20), misleading followers from the narrow path. They claim a restored priesthood through Smith in 1829, yet Hebrews 7:24 affirms Jesus’ unchangeable priesthood, needing no restoration. The belief in a premortal existence and eternal progression to godhood, taught in their scriptures, defies Isaiah 43:10, which says no gods exist before or after Jehovah. Elevating Smith as a prophet whose words add to the Bible ignores Deuteronomy 18:20’s warning against false prophets who lead away from Jehovah. The Book of Abraham, part of the Pearl of Great Price, claimed as Abraham’s writings, is proven by Egyptologists to be a 1st-century BCE funerary text, exposing Smith’s deception. Their doctrine of a Heavenly Mother introduces goddess worship, lacking Scriptural support and clashing with Deuteronomy 6:4’s monotheism. Mormonism’s teachings lead followers away from Jehovah’s truth, urging us to choose His pure worship as we climb with faith.
