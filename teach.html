THE FUTURE

1. Current events and attitudes indicate a change is near.—Matt. 24:3, 7, 8; Luke 21:10, 11; 2 Tim. 3:1-5.

The Bible’s description of the last days matches what we see today—wars, famines, earthquakes, and a rise in lawlessness. These events, combined with a global preaching work, fulfill Jesus’ prophecy about the sign of his presence. Attitudes have also shifted, with many becoming lovers of themselves, money, and pleasure rather than lovers of God, showing a decline in moral values as foretold.

"While he was sitting on the Mount of Olives, the disciples approached him privately, saying: 'Tell us, when will these things be, and what will be the sign of your presence and of the conclusion of the system of things?' ... 'For nation will rise against nation and kingdom against kingdom, and there will be food shortages and earthquakes in one place after another. All these things are a beginning of pangs of distress.'" - Matthew 24:3, 7, 8

"Then he said to them: 'Nation will rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom. There will be great earthquakes, and in one place after another food shortages and pestilences; and there will be fearful sights and from heaven great signs.'" - Luke 21:10, 11

THE FUTURE

2. The earth will never be destroyed.—Ps. 104:5; Eccl. 1:4.

God created the earth to be a permanent home for humans. His purpose has not changed, even with the challenges we face today. The Scriptures emphasize that the earth will remain, and God will ensure it is a suitable dwelling place for those who love him, fulfilling his original intention for mankind to live on it forever.

"He has established the earth on its foundations; it will not be moved from its place forever and ever." - Psalm 104:5

"A generation is going, and a generation is coming, but the earth remains forever." - Ecclesiastes 1:4

THE FUTURE

3. The earth’s environment will be restored.—Isa. 35:1, 2; Rev. 11:18.

The Bible describes a time when deserts will blossom and streams will flow in dry lands, a stark contrast to today’s environmental degradation. God will also hold accountable those who ruin the earth, ensuring that his creation is healed and restored to its intended beauty for the enjoyment of those who live under his Kingdom.

"The wilderness and the parched land will exult, and the desert plain will be joyful and blossom as the saffron. It will blossom profusely and also rejoice with joy and shouting. The glory of Lebanon will be given to it, the splendor of Carmel and Sharon. They will see the glory of Jehovah, the splendor of our God." - Isaiah 35:1, 2

"But the nations became wrathful, and your own wrath came, and the appointed time came for the dead to be judged and to reward your slaves the prophets and the holy ones and those fearing your name, the small and the great, and to bring to ruin those ruining the earth." - Revelation 11:18

THE FUTURE

4. Everyone will have perfect health.—Isa. 33:24; 35:5, 6.

Under God’s Kingdom, the effects of sin, including sickness, will be removed. The blind will see, the deaf will hear, and the lame will walk, as the Bible promises. This restoration of health will allow people to enjoy life to the full, free from the pain and limitations that afflict humanity today.

"And no resident will say: 'I am sick.' The people dwelling in the land will be pardoned for their error." - Isaiah 33:24

"At that time the eyes of the blind will be opened, and the ears of the deaf will be unstopped. At that time the lame will leap like a deer, and the tongue of the speechless will shout for joy. For waters will burst forth in the wilderness, and streams in the desert plain." - Isaiah 35:5, 6

THE FUTURE

5. You can live forever on earth.—Ps. 37:29; Matt. 5:5.

God’s original purpose was for humans to live forever on earth, and he has not abandoned that plan. The meek, those who humbly submit to Jehovah’s will, will inherit the earth and enjoy everlasting life in peace and happiness, as promised in the Scriptures.

"The righteous will possess the earth, and they will live forever on it." - Psalm 37:29

"Happy are the mild-tempered, since they will inherit the earth." - Matthew 5:5

FAMILY

6. A husband should “love his wife as he does himself.”—Eph. 5:33; Col. 3:19.

The Bible instructs husbands to show self-sacrificing love, putting their wife’s needs ahead of their own, just as Christ loved the congregation. This love involves tenderness, understanding, and support, ensuring that the wife feels cherished and secure in the marriage.

"Nevertheless, each one of you must love his wife as he does himself; on the other hand, the wife should have deep respect for her husband." - Ephesians 5:33

"You husbands, keep on loving your wives and do not be bitterly angry with them." - Colossians 3:19

FAMILY

7. A wife should deeply respect her husband.—Eph. 5:33; Col. 3:18.

Respect involves honoring the husband’s role as head of the family, supporting his decisions, and showing appreciation for his efforts. This creates a harmonious partnership where both husband and wife feel valued and work together to build a loving family.

"Nevertheless, each one of you must love his wife as he does himself; on the other hand, the wife should have deep respect for her husband." - Ephesians 5:33

"You wives, be in subjection to your husbands, as it is becoming in the Lord." - Colossians 3:18

FAMILY

8. A husband and a wife should be loyal to each other.—Mal. 2:16; Matt. 19:4-6, 9; Heb. 13:4.

God views marriage as a sacred bond, a union that should not be broken by infidelity or abandonment. Loyalty means remaining faithful in thought, word, and action, ensuring that the marriage remains a safe and secure refuge for both partners.

"For I hate divorce,” says Jehovah the God of Israel, “and the one who covers his garment with violence,” says Jehovah of armies. “So guard yourselves respecting your spirit, and do not deal treacherously." - Malachi 2:16

"In reply he said: 'Have you not read that the one who created them from the beginning made them male and female and said, ‘For this reason a man will leave his father and his mother and will stick to his wife, and the two will be one flesh’? So that they are no longer two, but one flesh. Therefore, what God has yoked together, let no man put apart.' ... I say to you that whoever divorces his wife, except on the grounds of sexual immorality, and marries another commits adultery." - Matthew 19:4-6, 9

FAMILY

9. Children who respect and obey their parents will succeed.—Prov. 1:8, 9; Eph. 6:1-3.

Children who listen to their parents’ guidance gain wisdom and avoid many pitfalls in life. Obedience to parents, as long as their instructions align with God’s principles, brings blessings, including a long and fulfilling life, as the Bible promises.

"Listen, my son, to the discipline of your father, and do not forsake the instruction of your mother. For they are a garland of favor for your head and a fine necklace for your throat." - Proverbs 1:8, 9

"Children, be obedient to your parents in union with the Lord, for this is righteous. 'Honor your father and your mother' is the first command with a promise: 'That it may go well with you and you may remain a long time on the earth.'" - Ephesians 6:1-3

GOD

10. God has a name.—Ps. 83:18; Jer. 10:10.

God’s name, Jehovah, appears thousands of times in ancient Bible manuscripts. Using his name shows that we recognize him as a real person, not an abstract force, and it honors him as the Creator who desires a close relationship with us.

"May people know that you, whose name is Jehovah, you alone are the Most High over all the earth." - Psalm 83:18

"But Jehovah is truly God. He is the living God and the eternal King. Because of his indignation the earth will quake, and no nations will endure his denunciation." - Jeremiah 10:10

GOD

11. God communicates with us.—2 Tim. 3:16, 17; 2 Pet. 1:20, 21.

The Bible is God’s inspired Word, written by human servants under the direction of his holy spirit. It contains his thoughts, principles, and promises, equipping us to make wise decisions and live in a way that pleases him.

"All Scripture is inspired of God and beneficial for teaching, for reproving, for setting things straight, for disciplining in righteousness, so that the man of God may be fully competent, completely equipped for every good work." - 2 Timothy 3:16, 17

"For you know this first, that no prophecy of Scripture springs from any private interpretation. For prophecy was at no time brought by man’s will, but men spoke from God as they were moved by holy spirit." - 2 Peter 1:20, 21

GOD

12. God is fair and unbiased.—Deut. 10:17; Acts 10:34, 35.

Jehovah does not show favoritism based on race, nationality, or social standing. He looks at a person’s heart, their faith, and their actions, ensuring that all who seek him with sincerity are accepted and blessed by him.

"For Jehovah your God is the God of gods and the Lord of lords, the God great, mighty, and awe-inspiring, who does not show favoritism or take a bribe." - Deuteronomy 10:17

"At this Peter began to speak, and he said: 'Now I truly understand that God is not partial, but in every nation the man who fears him and does what is right is acceptable to him.'" - Acts 10:34, 35

GOD

13. God wants to help us.—Ps. 46:1; 145:18, 19.

Jehovah is described as a refuge and strength, always ready to assist those who call on him. He listens to the prayers of the righteous and fulfills their heartfelt desires, providing comfort and guidance in times of need.

"God is our refuge and strength, a help that is readily found in times of distress." - Psalm 46:1

"Jehovah is near to all those calling on him, to all who call on him in truth. He satisfies the desire of those who fear him; he hears their cry for help and saves them." - Psalm 145:18, 19

PRAYER

14. God wants us to pray to him.—Ps. 62:8; 65:2; 1 Pet. 5:7.

Prayer is an expression of trust in Jehovah, a way to pour out our hearts to him. He invites us to cast our anxieties on him, assuring us that he hears and cares for us, strengthening our faith and drawing us closer to him.

"Trust in him at all times, O people; pour out your hearts before him. God is a refuge for us." - Psalm 62:8

"O Hearer of prayer, to you people of all sorts will come." - Psalm 65:2

PRAYER

15. The Bible teaches us how to pray.—Matt. 6:7-13; Luke 11:1-4.

Jesus taught his followers to pray with sincerity, focusing on God’s will, our daily needs, forgiveness, and protection from temptation. The model prayer emphasizes keeping God’s name holy and seeking his Kingdom first, showing us the proper priorities in prayer.

"When praying, do not say the same things over and over again as the people of the nations do, for they imagine they will get a hearing for their use of many words. So do not be like them, for your Father knows what you need even before you ask him. You must pray, then, this way: 'Our Father in the heavens, let your name be sanctified. Let your Kingdom come. Let your will take place, as in heaven, also on earth. Give us today our bread for this day; and forgive us our debts, as we also have forgiven our debtors. And do not bring us into temptation, but deliver us from the wicked one.'" - Matthew 6:7-13

"Now he was in a certain place praying, and when he stopped, one of his disciples said to him: 'Lord, teach us how to pray, just as John also taught his disciples.' So he said to them: 'Whenever you pray, say: ‘Father, let your name be sanctified. Let your Kingdom come. Give us our bread for this day according to our daily need. And forgive us our sins, for we ourselves also forgive everyone who is in debt to us; and do not bring us into temptation.’'" - Luke 11:1-4

PRAYER

16. We should pray often.—Matt. 7:7, 8; 1 Thess. 5:17.

The Bible encourages us to pray persistently and without ceasing, making prayer a constant part of our lives. This habit helps us maintain a close relationship with Jehovah, relying on him for guidance and strength in all circumstances.

"Keep on asking, and it will be given you; keep on seeking, and you will find; keep on knocking, and it will be opened to you; for everyone asking receives, and everyone seeking finds, and to everyone knocking, it will be opened." - Matthew 7:7, 8

"Pray constantly." - 1 Thessalonians 5:17

JESUS

17. Jesus was a great teacher whose advice always works.—Matt. 6:14, 15, 34; 7:12.

Jesus’ teachings, such as the Golden Rule and advice on forgiveness, are timeless principles that lead to peace and happiness. His counsel to avoid unnecessary anxiety by focusing on today’s needs helps us trust in Jehovah’s care and live with a positive outlook.

"For if you forgive men their trespasses, your heavenly Father will also forgive you; whereas if you do not forgive men their trespasses, neither will your Father forgive your trespasses." - Matthew 6:14, 15

"All things, therefore, that you want men to do to you, you also must do to them. This, in fact, is what the Law and the Prophets mean." - Matthew 7:12

JESUS

18. Jesus foretold events we see today.—Matt. 24:3, 7, 8, 14; Luke 21:10, 11.

Jesus predicted wars, food shortages, earthquakes, and the global preaching of the good news as signs of the last days. These events, along with the spread of the Kingdom message worldwide, confirm that we are living in the time he spoke about, nearing the end of this system.

"While he was sitting on the Mount of Olives, the disciples approached him privately, saying: 'Tell us, when will these things be, and what will be the sign of your presence and of the conclusion of the system of things?' ... 'For nation will rise against nation and kingdom against kingdom, and there will be food shortages and earthquakes in one place after another. All these things are a beginning of pangs of distress.' ... And this good news of the Kingdom will be preached in all the inhabited earth for a witness to all the nations, and then the end will come." - Matthew 24:3, 7, 8, 14

"Then he said to them: 'Nation will rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom. There will be great earthquakes, and in one place after another food shortages and pestilences; and there will be fearful sights and from heaven great signs.'" - Luke 21:10, 11

JESUS

19. Jesus is God’s Son.—Matt. 16:16; John 3:16; 1 John 4:15.

Jesus’ role as God’s Son highlights his unique relationship with Jehovah. He was sent to earth to give his life as a ransom, making it possible for humans to be reconciled to God and gain everlasting life through faith in him.

"Simon Peter answered: 'You are the Christ, the Son of the living God.'" - Matthew 16:16

"For God loved the world so much that he gave his only-begotten Son, so that everyone exercising faith in him might not be destroyed but have everlasting life." - John 3:16

JESUS

20. Jesus is not God Almighty.—John 14:28; 1 Cor. 11:3.

Jesus himself acknowledged that the Father is greater than he is, showing his submission to Jehovah. The Bible also describes Jesus as having a head, Jehovah, indicating a clear hierarchy and refuting the idea that Jesus is equal to God Almighty.

"You heard that I said to you, ‘I am going away and I am coming back to you.’ If you loved me, you would rejoice that I am going to the Father, for the Father is greater than I am." - John 14:28

"But I want you to know that the head of every man is the Christ; in turn, the head of a woman is the man; in turn, the head of the Christ is God." - 1 Corinthians 11:3

GOD’S KINGDOM

21. God’s Kingdom is a real government in heaven.—Dan. 2:44; 7:13, 14; Matt. 6:9, 10; Rev. 11:15.

God’s Kingdom, with Jesus as its appointed King, is a heavenly government that will rule over the earth. It will bring peace, justice, and righteousness, fulfilling Jehovah’s purpose for humanity as expressed in the model prayer taught by Jesus.

"In the days of those kings the God of heaven will set up a kingdom that will never be destroyed. And this kingdom will not be passed on to any other people. It will crush and put an end to all these kingdoms, and it alone will stand forever." - Daniel 2:44

"You must pray, then, this way: 'Our Father in the heavens, let your name be sanctified. Let your Kingdom come. Let your will take place, as in heaven, also on earth.'" - Matthew 6:9, 10

GOD’S KINGDOM

22. God’s Kingdom will replace human governments.—Ps. 2:7-9; Dan. 2:44.

Human governments, despite their efforts, cannot solve mankind’s problems. The Bible foretells that God’s Kingdom will crush all human rulerships and establish a perfect government that will rule forever, bringing true peace and security to the earth.

"Let me proclaim the decree of Jehovah; he said to me: 'You are my son; today I have become your father. Ask of me, and I will give nations as your inheritance and the ends of the earth as your possession. You will break them with an iron scepter, and you will smash them like a piece of pottery.'" - Psalm 2:7-9

"In the days of those kings the God of heaven will set up a kingdom that will never be destroyed. And this kingdom will not be passed on to any other people. It will crush and put an end to all these kingdoms, and it alone will stand forever." - Daniel 2:44

GOD’S KINGDOM

23. God’s Kingdom is the only solution to mankind’s problems.—Ps. 37:10, 11; 46:9; Isa. 65:21-23.

Under God’s Kingdom, war, poverty, and injustice will be eliminated. People will build houses, plant vineyards, and enjoy the work of their hands in peace, as Jehovah’s rule ensures that all of humanity’s needs are met in a perfect and just way.

"Just a little while longer, and the wicked will be no more; you will look at where they were, and they will not be there. But the meek will possess the earth, and they will find exquisite delight in the abundance of peace." - Psalm 37:10, 11

"They will build houses and live in them, and they will plant vineyards and eat their fruitage. They will not build for someone else to inhabit, nor will they plant for others to eat. For the days of my people will be like the days of a tree, and the work of their hands my chosen ones will enjoy to the full." - Isaiah 65:21-22

SUFFERING

24. God does not cause our suffering.—Deut. 32:4; Jas. 1:13.

Jehovah is a God of justice and love, incapable of causing evil or suffering. The Bible explains that suffering often results from human imperfection, the influence of Satan, or time and unforeseen events, but Jehovah is not to blame for our trials.

"The Rock, perfect is his activity, for all his ways are justice. A God of faithfulness who is never unjust; righteous and upright is he." - Deuteronomy 32:4

"When under trial, let no one say: 'I am being tried by God.' For with evil things God cannot be tried, nor does he himself try anyone." - James 1:13

SUFFERING

25. Satan rules this world.—Luke 4:5, 6; 1 John 5:19.

Satan has temporary authority over the world, using his influence to promote selfishness, violence, and injustice. This explains why suffering and wickedness are so widespread, as Satan opposes Jehovah’s righteous standards and seeks to mislead humanity.

"So he brought him up and showed him all the kingdoms of the inhabited earth in an instant of time. Then the Devil said to him: 'I will give you all this authority and their glory, because it has been handed over to me, and I give it to whomever I wish.'" - Luke 4:5, 6

"We know that we originate with God, but the whole world is lying in the power of the wicked one." - 1 John 5:19

SUFFERING

26. God cares about your suffering.—Ps. 34:17-19; Isa. 41:10, 13.

Jehovah is a compassionate God who hears the cries of those who suffer. He promises to be with us, strengthening and supporting us through our trials, and he assures us that he will never abandon those who trust in him.

"They cried out, and Jehovah heard; he rescued them from all their distresses. Jehovah is close to the brokenhearted; he saves those who are crushed in spirit." - Psalm 34:17-18

"Do not be afraid, for I am with you. Do not be anxious, for I am your God. I will fortify you, yes, I will help you, I will hold on to you with my right hand of righteousness." - Isaiah 41:10

SUFFERING

27. God will soon end suffering.—Isa. 65:17; Rev. 21:3, 4.

In the new world, Jehovah will wipe out every tear, and suffering will be a thing of the past. This promise gives hope to those enduring hardships, assuring them that God’s Kingdom will bring an end to all forms of pain and sorrow.

"For look! I am creating new heavens and a new earth; and the former things will not be called to mind, nor will they come up into the heart." - Isaiah 65:17

"With that I heard a loud voice from the throne say: 'Look! The tent of God is with mankind, and he will reside with them, and they will be his peoples. And God himself will be with them. And he will wipe out every tear from their eyes, and death will be no more, neither will mourning nor outcry nor pain be anymore. The former things have passed away.'" - Revelation 21:3, 4

DEATH

28. The dead are unconscious; they are not suffering.—Eccl. 9:5; John 11:11-14.

The Bible compares death to sleep, a state of complete unconsciousness where the dead cannot think, feel, or act. This understanding brings comfort, as it means our loved ones who have died are not suffering in any way.

"For the living know that they will die, but the dead know nothing at all, nor do they have any more reward, because all memory of them is forgotten." - Ecclesiastes 9:5

"After he said these things, he added: 'Lazarus our friend has fallen asleep, but I am traveling there to awaken him.' ... Then Jesus said to them plainly: 'Lazarus has died.'" - John 11:11, 14

DEATH

29. The dead cannot help us or harm us.—Ps. 146:4; Eccl. 9:6, 10.

The dead have no ability to influence the living, as they are unconscious and their emotions and actions have ceased. This truth protects us from false beliefs about spirits or the dead, encouraging us to rely on Jehovah for help and guidance.

"His spirit goes out, he returns to the ground; on that very day his thoughts perish." - Psalm 146:4

"Also, their love and their hate and their jealousy have already perished, and they no longer have any share in what is done under the sun." - Ecclesiastes 9:6

DEATH

30. Dead loved ones will be resurrected.—Job 14:13-15; John 5:28, 29; Acts 24:15.

Jehovah has the power to bring the dead back to life, and he longs to do so. The resurrection will reunite families, giving people the opportunity to live forever on a paradise earth, free from the pain of death.

"O that in the Grave you would conceal me, that you would hide me until your anger passes by, that you would set a time limit for me and remember me! If a man dies, can he live again? I will wait all the days of my compulsory service until my relief comes." - Job 14:13-14

"Do not be amazed at this, for the hour is coming in which all those in the memorial tombs will hear his voice and come out, those who did good things to a resurrection of life, and those who practiced vile things to a resurrection of judgment." - John 5:28, 29

DEATH

31. “Death will be no more.”—Rev. 21:3, 4; Isa. 25:8.

In the new world, death will be swallowed up forever, and people will live without the fear of losing loved ones. This promise assures us that Jehovah’s purpose for humans to live forever will be fully realized under his Kingdom.

"With that I heard a loud voice from the throne say: 'Look! The tent of God is with mankind, and he will reside with them, and they will be his peoples. And God himself will be with them. And he will wipe out every tear from their eyes, and death will be no more, neither will mourning nor outcry nor pain be anymore. The former things have passed away.'" - Revelation 21:3, 4

"He will swallow up death forever, and the Sovereign Lord Jehovah will wipe away the tears from all faces. The reproach of his people he will take away from all the earth, for Jehovah himself has spoken." - Isaiah 25:8

RELIGION

32. Not all religions please God.—Jer. 7:11; Matt. 7:13, 14, 21-23.

Many religions fail to teach the truth about Jehovah and his purposes, often promoting false doctrines or practices that dishonor him. The Bible urges us to worship God in a way that aligns with his standards, as only true worship leads to life.

"Has this house that bears my name become a cave of robbers in your eyes? Here I have seen it myself,' declares Jehovah." - Jeremiah 7:11

"Go in through the narrow gate, because broad is the gate and spacious is the road leading off into destruction, and many are going in through it; whereas narrow is the gate and cramped the road leading off into life, and few are finding it." - Matthew 7:13, 14

RELIGION

33. God hates hypocrisy.—Isa. 29:13; Mic. 3:11; Mark 7:6-8.

Hypocrisy involves outward acts of worship that do not reflect a genuine love for Jehovah. The Bible warns against following human traditions that contradict God’s Word, as true worship requires a heart fully devoted to him.

"Jehovah says: 'This people approach me with their mouth and they honor me with their lips, but their heart is far removed from me; and their fear of me is based on commands of men that they have been taught.'" - Isaiah 29:13

"He said to them: 'Isaiah aptly prophesied about you hypocrites, as it is written, ‘This people honor me with their lips, but their hearts are far removed from me. It is in vain that they keep worshipping me, for they teach commands of men as doctrines.’ You let go of the commandment of God and cling to the tradition of men.'" - Mark 7:6-8

RELIGION

34. Genuine love identifies true religion.—Mic. 4:3; John 13:34, 35.

True worshippers demonstrate love by living peaceably and showing self-sacrificing love for one another. This love transcends national and cultural boundaries, uniting people in a global brotherhood that reflects Jehovah’s own loving nature.

"He will render judgment among many peoples and set matters straight respecting mighty nations far away. They will beat their swords into plowshares and their spears into pruning shears. Nation will not lift up sword against nation, nor will they learn war anymore." - Micah 4:3

"I am giving you a new commandment, that you love one another; just as I have loved you, you also love one another. By this all will know that you are my disciples—if you have love among yourselves." - John 13:34, 35
