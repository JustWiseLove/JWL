TRUSTWORTHY

The Gift of Life

Every breath you take is a gift from Jehovah, the one who sparked life itself. No matter what you’ve faced, your existence is proof of his love, a reason to look up with gratitude.

The Bible reveals, “Know that Jehovah is God. It is he who made us” (Psalm 100:3). In the beginning, around 4026 BCE, he crafted humanity: “The true God himself made the earth and its inhabitants” (Isaiah 45:18). Your life isn’t random—it’s his design, meant to shine: “Everything he has made pretty in its time” (Ecclesiastes 3:11).

Jehovah’s gift outlasts worldly shadows. He formed you to know him, declaring, “I am Jehovah, your Maker” (Isaiah 54:5). Because he gave you life, you’re never alone, as “his invisible qualities are clearly seen from the world’s creation onward” (Romans 1:20).

“It is he who made us.” —Psalm 100:3

Crafted for Joy

Life isn’t just existence—Jehovah made it to be joyful, shaped for a purpose that lifts your soul. You were created to reflect his beauty and find delight in him.

Scripture says, “Rejoice in Jehovah, you righteous ones” (Psalm 97:12). Jehovah designed humans to mirror his love and wisdom: “Jehovah himself gives wisdom; from his mouth come knowledge and discernment” (Proverbs 2:6). He intended life to be rich, as “happy is the people whose God is Jehovah” (Psalm 144:15).

Even if joy feels distant, Jehovah’s purpose endures. He invites you to serve him gladly: “Serve Jehovah with rejoicing” (Psalm 100:2). By living for him, you find what life’s meant for, as “the one offering praise glorifies me” (Psalm 50:23).

“Serve Jehovah with rejoicing.” —Psalm 100:2

Nourished by Grace

Jehovah doesn’t just give life—he sustains it, filling the earth with gifts to keep you strong. His care shows he values every moment you live.

The Bible notes, “He makes grass grow for the cattle, and vegetation for man’s use” (Psalm 104:14). Jehovah’s earth provides: “You open your hand and satisfy the desire of every living thing” (Psalm 145:16). From harvest to sunrise, he ensures “the earth is satisfied with the fruit of your works” (Psalm 104:13).

His grace never falters. When Elijah needed strength, Jehovah sent food: “Get up and eat” (1 Kings 19:5). For you, his provision—seen in nature’s bounty—reminds you life is precious, as “he did good, giving you rains from heaven” (Acts 14:17).

“You satisfy the desire of every living thing.” —Psalm 145:16

Taught by Wisdom

Life thrives when guided by Jehovah’s wisdom. His teachings show you how to live well, making every day a step closer to him.

Scripture declares, “The fear of Jehovah is the beginning of wisdom” (Proverbs 9:10). His commands light the way: “I have come to know that everything the true God does will endure forever” (Ecclesiastes 3:14). Moses learned this, urging, “Apply your hearts to all the words that I am speaking” (Deuteronomy 32:46).

Worldly paths confuse, but Jehovah’s truth clarifies. He offers, “If any one of you is lacking in wisdom, let him keep asking God” (James 1:5). By studying his Word, you gain life’s direction, as “happy is the man who finds wisdom” (Proverbs 3:13).

“Happy is the man who finds wisdom.” —Proverbs 3:13

Redeemed by Love

Sin could have ended life’s hope, but Jesus’ love changed that. His sacrifice bridges you back to Jehovah, making life eternal possible.

Jesus gave everything: “No one has love greater than this, that someone should surrender his life for his friends” (John 15:13). In 33 CE, he died for us: “By means of him we have the release by ransom” (Ephesians 1:7). His blood restores what Adam lost, as “he who sent me wants me to lose nothing” (John 6:39).

Whatever your past, Jesus’ act cleanses: “You were bought with a price” (1 Corinthians 6:20). His love means you’re valued, free to live for Jehovah, as “we have boldness in the presence of God” (1 John 4:17).

“No one has love greater than this.” —John 15:13

Promised New Life

Life now is precious, but Jehovah offers more—eternal life through Jesus. His promise means death isn’t the end, but a step to forever.

Jesus pledged, “This is the will of my Father, that everyone who recognizes the Son and exercises faith in him should have everlasting life” (John 6:40). He showed it, raising Jairus’ daughter: “Little girl, I say to you, get up!” (Mark 5:41). Jehovah’s plan shines: “He will wipe out every tear from their eyes” (Revelation 21:4).

This hope fuels you. Like Paul, trust, “The gift God gives is everlasting life” (Romans 6:23). Jesus’ power ensures you’ll live again, as “all those in the tombs will come out” (John 5:28).

“Everyone who exercises faith should have everlasting life.” —John 6:40

Devoted to Our Maker

Jehovah gave you life; Jesus saved it. The best response is devotion—living every day to honor the one who made you, not chasing empty praise.

The Bible urges, “Give thanks to Jehovah, for he is good” (Psalm 106:1). Jesus modeled this: “I seek the will of him who sent me” (John 5:30). Your life can reflect that, as “whatever you do in word or in deed, do everything in the name of the Lord Jesus” (Colossians 3:17).

Devotion outweighs worldly gain. Choose Jehovah’s way: “The one doing the will of God remains forever” (1 John 2:17). Like Mary, who sat at Jesus’ feet, prioritize him: “Mary chose the good portion” (Luke 10:42).

“Do everything in the name of the Lord Jesus.” —Colossians 3:17

Thriving in Faith

You’re alive because Jehovah chose it, and Jesus secured it. Thrive in their love, letting faith shape a life that sings their praise, no matter what came before.

Jehovah’s love fuels you: “See what sort of love the Father has given us” (1 John 3:1). Jesus prayed, “I made your name known to them” (John 17:26), showing how to live fully. Faith makes life vibrant: “The one who has the Son has this life” (1 John 5:12).

Every act—loving, serving—honors your life-giver. Embrace it: “Rejoice and leap for joy” (Luke 6:23). Your faith proves you’re truly alive, as “the righteous one will live by reason of faith” (Hebrews 10:38).

“The one who has the Son has this life.” —1 John 5:12

Alive and Powerful

“For the word of God is alive and exerts power and is sharper than any two-edged sword and pierces even to the dividing of soul and spirit, and of joints and their marrow, and is able to discern thoughts and intentions of the heart.” —Hebrews 4:12
