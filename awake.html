AWAKE

Hope for the Broken

Life’s hardships—addiction, loss, or pain—can spark change, leading you to Jehovah’s truth. The Bible offers hope to the broken, promising strength to rise above. “Jehovah is close to the brokenhearted,” saving those crushed in spirit (Psalm 34:18).

Rahab, in a pagan city, chose Jehovah, becoming Jesus’ ancestor (Joshua 2:11; Matthew 1:5). Unlike Israel, who forgot God in prosperity (2 Kings 17:8), your trials push you to seek better. Jesus invites, “Come to me, all you who are toiling… and I will refresh you” (Matthew 11:28).

Embrace Jehovah’s promise of “a new heart” (Ezekiel 36:26). Your rough past fuels transformation, leading to a life awake in his purpose, with hope for “new heavens and a new earth” (2 Peter 3:13).

“Jehovah is close to the brokenhearted.” —Psalm 34:18

Strength in Trials

Trials test faith, but enduring them strengthens your trust in Jehovah, unlike the untested who drift without purpose. Job’s story shows suffering, met with faith, brings reward (Job 1:21; 42:10).

Job lost everything yet said, “Until I expire, I will not renounce my integrity” (Job 27:5). His endurance proved Satan wrong (Job 1:9). “Tribulation produces endurance” (Romans 5:3), and Jehovah promises, “The one who endures to the end will be saved” (Matthew 24:13).

Unlike complacent Israel (Hosea 13:6), your hardships forge resilience. Trust Jehovah, who sees your heart and vows, “I will make you wise” (Psalm 51:6), guiding you to lasting peace.

“Until I expire, I will not renounce my integrity.” —Job 27:5

Healing Anger’s Scars

Pain from a rough past often fuels anger, but the Bible shows Jehovah’s way to heal, turning rage into peace. “A wise person keeps it in check,” unlike a fool who vents (Proverbs 29:11).

Cain’s unchecked anger led to sin (Genesis 4:7), but David spared Saul, honoring Jehovah (1 Samuel 26:11). “Be agitated, but do not sin… keep silent” (Psalm 4:4). Jesus warned, “Everyone wrathful with his brother” faces judgment (Matthew 5:22).

Pray like Nehemiah (Nehemiah 2:4) to find “the peace of God that surpasses all understanding” (Philippians 4:7). Jehovah’s truth heals wounds, freeing you to live with love and calm.

“A wise person keeps it in check.” —Proverbs 29:11

Raising Faithful Hearts

If your childhood lacked care, you’re driven to raise your children better, rooted in Jehovah’s truth. The Bible guides you to break cycles of neglect with faith and love.

Jehovah commands, “Inculcate [my words] in your children” (Deuteronomy 6:6-7). Eunice taught Timothy “the holy writings” from infancy (2 Timothy 3:15). Unlike Eli, whose sons suffered for his failure (1 Samuel 3:12), guide firmly: “Train a boy in the way he should go” (Proverbs 22:6).

Your resolve gives children what you missed. Jehovah promises, “I will pour out my spirit on your descendants” (Isaiah 44:3), ensuring a faithful legacy through your love.

“Train a boy in the way he should go.” —Proverbs 22:6

Standing Firm in Loyalty

Faith can divide you from worldly family, but the Bible teaches loyalty to Jehovah while showing love. Abraham prayed for Lot despite his worldly choices (Genesis 13:5-13; 18:23-32), yet stayed true to God.

Eli’s failure to correct his sons’ wickedness brought judgment (1 Samuel 2:29). Jesus warned, “Whoever has greater affection for father or mother than for me is not worthy of me” (Matthew 10:37). Faith divides, as “a man [is] against his father” (Matthew 10:35).

Pray for family and uphold Jehovah’s standards. Like Abraham leaving Ur (Genesis 12:4), choose God’s path, shining as a light to draw kin to truth (Matthew 5:16).

“Whoever has greater affection… is not worthy of me.” —Matthew 10:37

Loving Like Jesus

Jesus showed how to love unbelieving family without compromising faith. When his brothers urged fame, he calmly focused on Jehovah’s will (John 7:3-10), and some later believed (Acts 1:14).

He prioritized spiritual ties—“Whoever does the will of my Father… is my brother” (Matthew 12:50)—yet cared for his mother (John 19:26-27). The prodigal son parable reflects hope for return (Luke 15:11-32). Jesus taught, “Continue to love your enemies” (Luke 6:35).

Be patient and kind, trusting your example may lead family to Jehovah. Stay focused on God’s purpose, showing love that mirrors Christ’s.

“Continue to love your enemies.” —Luke 6:35

Shining as God’s Family

The Bible equips you to live for Jehovah among worldly family, shining as “an example in speaking, conduct, love” (1 Timothy 4:12). Dinah’s worldly ties led to trouble (Genesis 34:1-2), warning us to guard faith (Psalm 1:1).

Jesus called faithful ones his family (Matthew 12:50), urging, “Make disciples of all the nations” (Matthew 28:19). Limit spiritual closeness with unbelievers (2 Corinthians 6:14), but show kindness: “Work what is good toward all” (Galatians 6:10).

Pray for strength—“the peace of God… will guard your hearts” (Philippians 4:6-7). Your joyful faith may draw family to Jehovah’s truth, as a light in darkness (Matthew 5:16).

“Be an example to the faithful ones.” —1 Timothy 4:12

Choosing Peace Over Strife

Arguments with worldly family drain joy, but the Bible teaches peace: “A mild answer turns away rage” (Proverbs 15:1). Be peaceable (Romans 12:18), avoiding “foolish debates” (2 Timothy 2:23).

Jesus withdrew from conflict (John 8:59). Listen patiently (James 1:19), speak graciously (Colossians 4:6), and redirect tense talks (Proverbs 17:14). If needed, withdraw politely (Psalm 4:4). Pray for wisdom—“the peace of God… will guard your hearts” (Philippians 4:6-7).

Prepare kind responses and set loving boundaries (2 Corinthians 6:14). By fleeing strife, you reflect Jehovah’s peace, keeping doors open for family to see his truth.

“A mild answer turns away rage.” —Proverbs 15:1

“For the word of God is alive and exerts power and is sharper than any two-edged sword and pierces even to the dividing of soul and spirit, and of joints and their marrow, and is able to discern thoughts and intentions of the heart.” —Hebrews 4:12
